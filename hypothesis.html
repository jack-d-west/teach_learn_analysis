Hypothesis Testing

Hypothesis testing is a statistical method used in data analytics to determine the validity of an assumption (the hypothesis) about a population based on sample data. It helps analysts make inferences and assess relationships, ultimately guiding data-driven decisions.

There are typically five steps in hypothesis testing:

  1. Formulating the null and alternative hypotheses.
  2. Choosing a significance level (Î±).
  3. Calculating a test statistic based on sample data.
  4. Determining the p-value or critical value to compare against the test statistic.
  5. Drawing a conclusion: reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Understanding hypothesis testing is crucial for evaluating relationships in data and validating models in analytics. This topic is divided into sessions covering different hypothesis testing methods and approaches.
